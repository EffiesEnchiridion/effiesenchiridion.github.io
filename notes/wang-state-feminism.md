# Wang Zheng, ‘“State Feminism”? Gender and Socialist State Formation in Maoist China’, Feminist Studies 31(3) (2005), pp. 519–551.

## ♠Point

1）socialist state ≠ a patriarchal and monolithic power that granted women certain rights from above, while women themselves had little agency

王政反对一种过于简单的理解：好像社会主义国家是一个统一的父权机器，由上而下地“赐予”女性一些权利，而女性自己没有真正的能动性，她指出先行研究的倾向性：  

In studies of feminism in capitalist democracies, scholars often identify the specific women actors who pushed gender-equality policies. But in studies of socialist China, pro-women policies are frequently treated as products of the state alone.  

关于妇联：  

Wang criticizes the common view that under Chinese “state feminism,” agency belonged exclusively to the party-state, which mobilized women through the ACWF while defining both the goals and methods of change.  

→Once the Women’s Federation is treated as nothing more than a state organ, women’s agency disappears from the story. Wang does not deny that the state was patriarchal, but she insists that this should not prevent us from seeing negotiation, intervention, and conflict within state processes.  

王政的核心观点：  

The Women’s Federation and the Party was never a simple story of domination and subordination. Women cadres were committed to socialist revolution, but that commitment did not prevent them from pursuing their own gendered visions of a new China.  

妇联和党的关系绝不是一个简单的“支配—服从”故事。妇联干部当然忠于社会主义革命，但这并不妨碍她们同时追求自己关于“新中国中的女性解放”的想象。

2）Wang suggests that although the CCP used women to display political support, women also appropriated state-sanctioned public space to cross gender and class boundaries and to stage their legitimacy in the new political order.  

→中共需要利用女性作为某种展示（或其他目的），与女性借助国家授权跨越了原本的性别和阶级边界并不冲突。

3）the conflict: women’s congress vs. residents committee: compete for cadres, constituents, tasks, and institutional authority  

The conflict was not just administrative overlap. It exposed a deeper issue: many male officials did not really recognize the necessity of an independent women’s organizational system.  

→The WF insisted women needed their own organizational channel and their own attention to “special issues.”  

*王政主要讲的是上海妇联在组织家庭妇女时和居委会产生的冲突，特别提到1953年，借着“整顿居民委员会”的机会，民政部门正式把妇代会界定成居民委员会之下的从属组织。其实上海妇联以家庭妇女为对象，本来就是女工阵地被工会把握后妇联重新调整对象的结果。结合我自己的史料调查，妇联其实在各种不同的工作中会和各种部门产生冲突（As different branches of the new regime negotiated their territories）。王政非常一针见血地指出这其实不仅仅是职能的重复来的，本质还是妇联作为一个女性团体的尴尬位置，很多干部（王政用了男性干部这个词，我觉得也不尽然，而且和性别身份的关系可能不深，这是一个长期的工作惯性）并不承认妇联的作用，或者说不愿意在工作中专门处理「妇女问题」。妇联一直争取，但及时能够参与，也一直被放在从属性的位置。  

→妇女干部的葛藤  

women cadres themselves raised critical questions: “What have women gained?” and what had been done as “special work for women”? They were aware of the imbalance between serving the Party and serving women.  

→王政的一个重要判断是：恰恰是在制度受挫之后，妇联反而更清楚地形成了一种以女性为中心的工作原则。她列举的：  

The WF argued that even if women’s organizations and residents committees shared many tasks, they did not share the same perspective. One cadre put it very clearly: “What distinguishes our work is only the perspective.” This perspective meant looking at childcare, domestic disputes, and community mobilization specifically from the standpoint of women’s and children’s rights.
